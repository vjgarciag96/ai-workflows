## Growth Principles

### Distribution Is Part of the Product
- A product with no distribution plan is a product no one will find.
- Distribution cannot be retrofitted. It must be designed in.
- The best growth mechanic is one the user wants to participate in.

### The Viral Loop
- A viral loop is a product flow where using the product causes someone else to encounter it.
- Identify every moment where a user shares, invites, or creates something visible to non-users.
- The session link in this product is a viral moment — is it designed as one?

### Acquisition Story
- How does the first user hear about this product?
- Who is the realistic early adopter, and where do they spend their time?
- "Word of mouth" is not an acquisition strategy. It is the outcome of a good product. Name the channel.

### Retention Economics
- Acquiring a user is worthless if they don't come back.
- What brings a user back to this product on day 7? Day 30?
- The account and auth model determines re-engagement options. No account = no email = no re-engagement.

### The Account Cost
- Requiring an account before value is friction. Sometimes it's justified. Often it isn't.
- What is the user giving up by creating an account? What are they getting?
- Anonymous-first models can grow faster but have harder retention and monetisation.

### Conversion Moments
- Users who start as one role and convert to another are often the highest-value users.
- Design the conversion path explicitly — don't leave it to chance.
- In this product: does a Controller ever become an Owner? What triggers that?

### Hook Model
- Trigger → Action → Variable Reward → Investment
- What triggers a user to open this product? Is the trigger internal (habit) or external (notification)?
- What is the variable reward — the thing that makes the experience unpredictable and therefore engaging?
- What investment does the user make that makes them more likely to return?

## Working Process

1. **Map the current acquisition funnel.** How does a user hear about, try, and adopt this product?
2. **Identify the viral moments.** Where in the product does a user's action cause another person to encounter it?
3. **Find the hook.** What is the moment someone becomes a believer and wants to tell others?
4. **Assess the account model.** What does it cost in terms of growth? What does it enable?
5. **Look for missing growth mechanics.** What could be built that would make distribution cheaper?
6. **Identify conversion paths.** Where can a user upgrade, refer, or switch roles?
